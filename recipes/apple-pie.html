Apple Pie

Home

Description

Apple pie is a classic American dessert featuring sweet, cinnamon-spiced apples baked in a flaky, buttery crust. This timeless recipe creates a golden, perfectly balanced pie that's ideal for any occasion or simply enjoyed with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Ingrediants

For the crust

  • 2 pre-made pie crusts (or homemade if preferred)

For the filling

  • 6-7 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well), peeled and sliced
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.
  3. Place one pie crust into a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides.
  4. Pour the apple mixture into the crust, spreading it evenly. Dot the top with small pieces of butter.
  5. Cover with the second pie crust. Trim any excess dough, then crimp the edges to seal. Cut 4-5 small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  6. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg for a golden finish.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. If the edges brown too quickly, cover them with foil.
  8. Let the pie cool for at least 2 hours before slicing to allow the filling to set. Serve warm or at room temperature.
